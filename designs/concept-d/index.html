Our Story

Some buildings are constructed. Others are composed. In 1927, architect Cass Gilbert — creator of the Woolworth Building and the United States Supreme Court — composed a masterpiece in brick, marble, and bronze for the coal country of eastern Pennsylvania. Nearly a century later, The Miners 1928 invites you to experience that composition anew — as a luxury hotel, a grand event venue, and a living monument to the age that built America.

Heritage

A Century in the Making

1828

The Beginning

The Miners Bank opens October 28th, eight months after Pottsville becomes a borough. One of the first banks in the anthracite region. It predates Yuengling Brewery by a year.

1830s–1920s

The Anthracite Era

Coal discovered on Sharp Mountain transforms the Schuylkill Valley. Irish, German, Welsh, and Eastern European immigrants arrive. Pottsville becomes the “City of Firsts” — first anthracite furnace, first Reading Railroad locomotive, first iron smelted with coal.

1927

Cass Gilbert's Masterwork

For the centennial, Cass Gilbert designs a masterwork. Harvard brick with four Doric columns of white Georgia marble. Keystones bearing Mercury, god of commerce, and Pluto, god of mines. Ten-foot brass doors by John Polachek depicting miners at work — his bronzes hang in the Smithsonian. The doors alone cost $10,000.

The Interior

A Cathedral of Commerce

A 50-foot vaulted ceiling with glass and bronze skylight. Twelve grand chandeliers. Walls of Jeanne d'Arc French limestone. Floors of Roman travertine, from the same quarries as the Colosseum. Black walnut counters with green marble tops. Fourteen types of marble. A 36-ton vault door of tool-proof steel — 27 inches thick, 24 bolts, four time locks.

2026

Reborn

Painstakingly restored to its original grandeur with modern luxury amenities woven seamlessly into the historic fabric. The Miners 1928 opens Spring 2026.
