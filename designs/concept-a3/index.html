The Building

A Century in the Making

The Miners National Bank opened in 1828, one of the earliest banks in the anthracite coal region. For its centennial, the bank commissioned Cass Gilbert — architect of the Woolworth Building and the U.S. Supreme Court — to create something extraordinary. The building opened November 7, 1927.

What Gilbert created is breathtaking: a 50-foot vaulted ceiling with a glass and bronze skylight, walls of French Jeanne d’Arc limestone, floors of Roman travertine — the same stone used in the Colosseum — fourteen types of marble, ten-foot brass entrance doors sculpted by John Polachek (whose work hangs in the Smithsonian), and a 36-ton vault door.

Now painstakingly restored, every original detail has been preserved while the building has been reimagined as Pottsville’s first luxury hotel and premier event destination. Opening Spring 2026.

Explore Pottsville

A historic coal-region town experiencing a quiet renaissance. Walk out the brass doors and discover what makes this corner of Pennsylvania unforgettable.

Yuengling Brewery

America’s oldest brewery, established 1829. Free tours daily. Walking distance from The Miners 1928.

Mahantongo Street

A mile of Victorian mansions built by coal barons at the height of the anthracite era. One of the finest residential streets in Pennsylvania.

Schuylkill River Trail

Scenic walking and cycling along the river that powered a region. Miles of maintained trail through the valley.

Historical Society

The Schuylkill County Historical Society chronicles the mining heritage, the people, and the industrial revolution that happened right here.

Sharp Mountain

Where anthracite coal was first discovered in America. The geological event that built this town and fueled a nation.

Downtown Pottsville

Local restaurants, independent shops, and a walkable downtown in the midst of a renaissance. Small-town character, real discoveries.
