The Building

A Cass Gilbert Masterpiece

Nearly two centuries of history. One extraordinary building.

The Miners National Bank was established in 1828—one of the earliest financial institutions in the anthracite region. When its centennial approached, the bank's leaders commissioned Cass Gilbert—the architect behind the Woolworth Building in New York City and the United States Supreme Court Building in Washington—to design a headquarters worthy of the occasion. The result opened on November 7, 1927, and was immediately recognized as one of the finest banking halls in the northeastern United States.

The building is a cathedral of American architecture: a soaring fifty-foot vaulted ceiling crowned by an original glass and bronze skylight. Walls of luminous French Jeanne d'Arc limestone. Floors of Roman travertine—the very stone used to build the Colosseum in Rome. Fourteen distinct types of marble. Twelve grand chandeliers on ornate medallions. Ten-foot brass doors sculpted by John Polachek—a master metalworker whose pieces are held by the Smithsonian Institution—depicting miners at their labor. And a thirty-six-ton vault door, twenty-seven inches of tool-proof steel, that once guarded the fortunes of the anthracite region.

Lovingly restored and beautifully reimagined. Every original detail preserved. Now open as a luxury hotel and event destination.

The Hotel

Thirty-Five Rooms.
Every One Brand New.

The building is a century-old masterpiece. The rooms? Built entirely from scratch.

Here is what makes The Miners 1928 unlike any hotel you've stayed in: the building is a century-old Cass Gilbert masterpiece, meticulously preserved. But the rooms? Every single one is built entirely from scratch—designed and constructed from the ground up within the historic shell. New walls. New electrical. New plumbing. New climate systems. New fixtures, new finishes, new everything.

The result is a collision of old and new that you can feel the moment you open the door: the patina of a century-old landmark, the comfort of a hotel that opened yesterday.

Your Destination

Your Destination Starts
at Our Front Door

Pottsville is a place worth knowing. Here's where to start.

Y

Yuengling Brewery

America's oldest brewery, established 1829. Free tours of the historic caves and taproom. Walking distance from The Miners 1928.

Your hotel is one year younger than America's oldest brewery—and just steps away.

M

Mahantongo Street

A boulevard of Victorian mansions built by coal barons at the height of the anthracite boom. Queen Anne, Romanesque, Second Empire architecture.

A living museum of Gilded Age ambition.

S

Schuylkill River Trail

Miles of scenic walking and cycling through the valley that coal built. Start your morning with a run along the river before breakfast.

The perfect way to earn your room service.

H

Schuylkill County Historical Society

A Victorian mansion museum with exhibits on mining heritage, immigrant communities, and the industrial revolution in anthracite country.

The stories behind the stone.

A

Sharp Mountain

Where Necho Allen first discovered anthracite coal in the 1790s—the discovery that built Pottsville and fueled a nation. Hiking and historical markers.

Stand where American industry began.

D

Downtown Pottsville

Restaurants, shops, galleries, and a walkable downtown that's experiencing a renaissance. The Miners 1928 is at its heart.

A town rediscovering itself—and well worth discovering yourself.
