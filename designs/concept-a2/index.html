The Building

A Masterpiece in Stone

Nearly two centuries of history. One extraordinary building.

Originally the Miners National Bank—established in 1828—this building was designed by Cass Gilbert, the architect of the Woolworth Building and the United States Supreme Court. Commissioned to mark the bank's centennial, it opened on November 7, 1927, to widespread celebration and was instantly recognized as one of the finest banking halls in the northeastern United States.

The interior is extraordinary: a fifty-foot vaulted ceiling crowned by an original glass and bronze skylight, walls of French Jeanne d’Arc limestone, floors of Roman travertine—the same stone used to build the Colosseum. Twelve grand chandeliers hang from ornate medallions. Fourteen types of marble create a palette no modern material can replicate. The ten-foot brass entrance doors, sculpted by John Polachek, depict miners at work—his bronzes hang in the Smithsonian. Behind the elegance stands a 36-ton vault door, forged from tool-proof steel 27 inches thick.

Now painstakingly restored and reimagined as a luxury hotel and celebration venue, every original detail has been preserved while modern amenities have been seamlessly woven in. The building that Pottsville once called “a temple of business and commerce” has become a temple of celebration.

Weddings

Your Wedding at The Miners 1928

This is not a ballroom with chandeliers. It is a Cass Gilbert masterpiece with a fifty-foot ceiling, a century-old skylight, and fourteen types of marble. Your wedding will be held in one of the most architecturally significant buildings in Pennsylvania.

Your Day, Reimagined

I

The Ceremony

Exchange vows in The Grand Hall beneath the original glass and bronze skylight. Fifty feet of vaulted grandeur above you. Twelve chandeliers glowing softly around you. Walls of French limestone. Floors of Roman travertine. No church, no garden, no ballroom can match this.

II

Cocktail Hour

Your guests move to The Gallery while The Grand Hall transforms. Craft cocktails from the built-in bar. Passed hors d’oeuvres. The intimate scale of The Gallery makes every conversation feel special.

III

The Reception

Return to The Grand Hall for dinner, toasts, and dancing. State-of-the-art lighting transforms the space. The sound system fills the room without competing with the architecture. Your first dance happens on Roman travertine, surrounded by fourteen types of marble and the soft glow of restored chandeliers.

IV

The After-Party

The celebration doesn’t have to end. With 30–35 luxury hotel rooms in the building, your guests are steps from their beds. No shuttles. No designated drivers. Just the elevator.
